LMBpultd - Integrated Farming

livestock, aquaculture, and crops for steady income and resource efficiency

    This project proposes a sustainable small-scale Integrated Farming System (IFS) on leased land in rural Ernakulam, Kerala, designed to generate steady income while ensuring ecological balance. The model integrates livestock such as cows, goats, poultry, quail, and rabbits with aquaculture including fish and crabs, alongside a wide range of crops such as vegetables, tubers, fruits, spices, and fodder. This diversity provides continuous production throughout the year, ensuring both food security and regular cash flow.

      Resource recycling is central to the system: animal manure is converted into organic fertilizer and biogas, enriching the soil and fishponds, while crop residues are used as livestock feed. This reduces dependence on external inputs and lowers operating costs. The farm structure balances short-term returns from vegetables and eggs, medium-term income from tubers, fish, and goats, and long-term gains from milk, fruits, and value-added products. With modern practices like drip irrigation, watershed management, and vermicomposting, the farm efficiently addresses soil and water challenges. Overall, the project is designed to be profitable, resilient, and environmentally friendly, supporting sustainable rural development while ensuring reliable income for repayment of capital investment.

Objectives

To establish a multi-crop and multi-livestock integrated farming unit.
To ensure steady income through staggered harvesting and livestock products.
To adopt sustainable practices: organic manure, biogas, watershed management.
To reduce dependence on external feed, fertilizer, and water by recycling resources.
To promote food and nutritional security through crop and livestock diversity.
To minimize risks by diversifying across crops, livestock, and aquaculture.
To encourage eco-friendly farming that conserves resources and environment.
